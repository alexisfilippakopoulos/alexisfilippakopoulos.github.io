Alexis Filippakopoulos

Distributed Messaging Application

Implements the Publish/Subscribe design pattern. Clients can connect to one of the three servers available; to communicate, create group chats, upload and view stories and videos, make friends, etc. Additionally, it implements consistent hashing, an event-driven architecture among the server nodes and synchronous data replication resulting in a fault tolerant system. Lastly, data storage takes place locally.

Developped with Java, Android Studio.

Invesigating the 1978 FIFA World Cup

Partly replicating Scharpf, A., Gläßel, C., Pearce, E. (2022) International Sports Events and Repression in Autocracies: Evidence from the 1978 FIFA World Cup., investigating the change in repression events as the tournament was closing in, under the argentinian jounta.

Developped with pandas, seaborn, statsmodels, ggplot, numpy, matplotlib, scipy.

The Decline of Disruptive Science

Drawing inspiration from this publication, I attempt to predict CD index (an index indicative of a paper's disruption) using an XGBooster and an instance of a BERT model for masked language modeling, using 1% of all CrossRef available publications that have abstracts, derived from the alexandria3k package.

Developped with pytorch, tensorflow, sklearn, pandas, sqlite, numpy.

Researching Economic Connectedness

Partly replicating Chetty, R., Jackson, M.O., Kuchler, T. et al. Social capital I: measurement and associations with economic mobility. Motif Extraction and Sequential Mining

Searching for the most frequent trigrams in an artificial messages dataset. Using and comparing the count, term frequency and inverse document frequency metrics. Finally, we focus on the conversation's ending messages and recreate R's TraMineR figures.

Developped with pandas, sklearn, seaborn, matplotlib.
